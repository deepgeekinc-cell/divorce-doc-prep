# Divorce Document Checklist - FindLaw

## Complete Document List for Initial Attorney Consultation

### Income & Tax Documents
- Individual income tax returns for the last three years
- Business income tax returns (if business owner)
- Paystubs for both spouses
- W-2s and 1099s

### Legal Agreements
- Prenuptial agreement
- Postnuptial agreement
- Separation agreement

### Financial Accounts
- Bank account statements (all accounts)
- Savings account statements
- Investment statements (CDs, stocks, bonds)
- Retirement account statements (IRA, 401(k), pension)
- Retirement account statements from before marriage

### Estate Planning Documents
- Powers of attorney paperwork
- Health care directives or living wills
- Trusts (for you or children)
- Other estate planning documents

### Property & Real Estate
- Registrations and titles (vehicles, boats, etc.)
- Most recent mortgage statements and documents
- Mortgage statements from property owned before marriage
- Property tax statements
- Real property appraisals
- Rental agreement (if renting)
- List of real estate owned before marriage

### Debts & Liabilities
- Credit card statements from each card
- Auto loans and statements
- Student loan information
- Other debts (medical bills, personal loans)

### Monthly Expenses
- Utility bills (cell phones, gas, electric, water)
- Child-related expenses (daycare, tuition, lessons, insurance, medications)
- Life insurance policies and costs
- Health insurance policies and costs
- Monthly budget worksheet
- Completed financial statements

### Personal Property
- Personal property appraisals (jewelry, heirlooms)
- List of personal property (furniture, jewelry, artwork, computers, electronics)
- List of contents of safety deposit boxes
- Any inheritance or significant gift received during marriage

### Credit & Other Documents
- Recent credit reports
- Government benefits documentation (social security, WIC, SNAP/food assistance)
- Any other legal documents that may be helpful

## Key Notes
- Not all documents are required for initial consultation
- Some may not apply to specific situations
- Uncontested divorces may require fewer documents
- Complete disclosure ensures fair property division
