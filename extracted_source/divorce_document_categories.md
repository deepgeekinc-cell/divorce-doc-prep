# Divorce Document Requirements - Comprehensive Research

## Overview

Divorce document requirements vary by state, but most states require similar categories of financial and legal documentation. The goal of this application is to help users gather all necessary documents before their first attorney meeting, potentially saving thousands in legal fees.

## Core Document Categories

### 1. Personal Identification & Marriage Documents
- Marriage certificate
- Birth certificates (for both spouses and children)
- Social Security cards
- Driver's licenses or state IDs
- Immigration documents (if applicable)

### 2. Income Documentation
- Last 3-5 years of federal, state, and local tax returns
- W-2 forms and 1099 forms
- Recent pay stubs (typically last 3-6 months)
- Business tax returns (if self-employed)
- Proof of bonuses, commissions, overtime pay
- Documentation of other income sources (rental income, royalties, etc.)
- Social Security benefits statements
- Disability or workers' compensation benefits
- Unemployment benefits documentation

### 3. Assets - Bank Accounts & Investments
- Bank account statements (checking, savings) - typically last 3-12 months
- Investment account statements (stocks, bonds, mutual funds)
- Certificates of deposit (CDs)
- Money market accounts
- Cryptocurrency holdings and wallet information
- Brokerage account statements
- Statements showing account balances before marriage (for separate property claims)

### 4. Retirement Accounts
- 401(k) statements
- IRA statements (traditional and Roth)
- Pension plan documents and statements
- 403(b) or 457 plan statements
- Deferred compensation plans
- Pre-marital retirement account statements

### 5. Real Estate & Property
- Mortgage statements and loan documents
- Property deeds and titles
- Property tax statements
- Home appraisals or assessments
- Rental property documentation
- Lease agreements (if renting)
- Home equity line of credit (HELOC) statements
- Second mortgages or refinancing documents

### 6. Vehicles & Personal Property
- Vehicle titles and registration
- Auto loan statements
- Vehicle appraisals or valuations
- Boat, RV, or motorcycle titles and loans
- Personal property appraisals (jewelry, art, collectibles, antiques)
- Inventory of household furnishings and electronics

### 7. Business Interests
- Business formation documents (LLC, corporation, partnership agreements)
- Business tax returns (3-5 years)
- Business financial statements (profit & loss, balance sheets)
- Business valuations
- Partnership or shareholder agreements
- Business bank account statements

### 8. Debts & Liabilities
- Credit card statements (all cards, last 6-12 months)
- Student loan statements and documentation
- Personal loan documents
- Auto loan statements
- Medical bills and payment plans
- Outstanding tax liabilities (IRS, state, local)
- Lines of credit
- Debts to family or friends
- Judgments or liens

### 9. Insurance Policies
- Life insurance policies (with cash value statements)
- Health insurance policies and costs
- Auto insurance policies
- Homeowners or renters insurance
- Disability insurance
- Long-term care insurance
- Umbrella policies

### 10. Legal Agreements
- Prenuptial agreements
- Postnuptial agreements
- Separation agreements
- Powers of attorney
- Health care directives or living wills
- Trust documents (for you or children)
- Estate planning documents (wills, beneficiary designations)

### 11. Monthly Expenses & Budget
- Utility bills (electric, gas, water, trash)
- Phone bills (cell and landline)
- Internet and cable bills
- Childcare expenses
- School tuition and fees
- Extracurricular activity costs
- Medical and dental expenses
- Prescription medication costs
- Grocery and household expenses
- Transportation costs
- Entertainment and recreation expenses
- Clothing expenses
- Pet care expenses
- Monthly budget worksheet or expense tracking

### 12. Child-Related Documents
- Child support orders (from previous relationships)
- Custody agreements (from previous relationships)
- School records and report cards
- Medical records
- Special needs documentation
- Childcare provider information and costs
- Health insurance information for children

### 13. Government Benefits
- Social Security statements
- SNAP/food assistance documentation
- WIC benefits
- Medicaid or Medicare information
- Veterans benefits
- Disability benefits (SSDI, SSI)

### 14. Credit & Financial Health
- Credit reports (from all three bureaus: Equifax, Experian, TransUnion)
- Credit scores
- Bankruptcy filings (if applicable)
- Foreclosure documents (if applicable)

### 15. Other Important Documents
- Contents of safety deposit boxes (inventory)
- Inheritance documentation
- Gift documentation (significant gifts during marriage)
- Lawsuit settlements or awards

## State-Specific Requirements

### Financial Affidavit/Disclosure Forms
Most states require completion of a **Financial Affidavit** or **Financial Disclosure Statement** that includes:
- Complete listing of all income sources
- Detailed inventory of all assets (separate and marital)
- Complete listing of all debts and liabilities
- Monthly living expenses

The threshold for "short form" vs "long form" varies by state:
- Connecticut: Less than $75,000 in income and assets uses short form
- Other states have different thresholds

### Mandatory Disclosure Timeline
States have different timelines for financial disclosure:
- Some require disclosure within 30-60 days of filing
- Others require it before any settlement discussions
- California has specific preliminary and final disclosure requirements

### Community Property vs Equitable Distribution States
**Community Property States** (9 states): Arizona, California, Idaho, Louisiana, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas, Washington, Wisconsin
- All property acquired during marriage is presumed community property (50/50 split)
- Separate property must be clearly documented

**Equitable Distribution States** (remaining states)
- Property divided fairly but not necessarily equally
- Courts consider multiple factors including length of marriage, earning capacity, contributions
